Velatura — the pellucid register
Lightest four grounds. Transparency depth over Cloud Dancer — barely a surface. Document headers, title fields, cover sheets.
07
Velatura
A thin transparent glaze applied over a dried ground. Each colour is a velatura over Cloud Dancer — the hue modifies without concealing. Used where paper must remain the dominant presence.

One colour from each depth band. Cloud Dancer as surface, Laitance as resolved near-surface, Usquebaugh as shallow deposit, Deckle as deep layer, Deckle-dim as bedrock. The distance arithmetic is stratigraphy.

Conspectus · closing note · P.10
A general survey. The supply of colour has always been in excess of the demand — the grammar enforces scarcity.
From the Billings Phenomenon: as scarce as truth is, the supply has always been in excess of the demand. The Nacre palette enacts the inverse — eight grounds, three inks, four proposals, each a restricted sub-grammar. The constant ink (Stretch Limo) holds the reference; the tinted inks (Grape-bg, Cosmic-bg) carry register. The system is closed.

P.11 · Abalone & Mixed Inks · v2.35 · Nacre sub-palette with mixed inks

Abalone
& Mixed Inks

The same nine nacre lights that are grounds on Cloud Dancer become luminous accents against the three dark inks. On paper they receive ink. On darkness they emit light. Same tokens, opposite semantic roles — the Abalone inversion.

Grammar rule · the Abalone inversion
"The same colour that is paper on Cloud Dancer becomes light on the dark ink. The hue does not change. The ground does."
In P.07–P.10, nacre lights are grounds — opaque, receiving the Stretch Limo or Grape-Black ink. In P.11, the dark inks are the ground and the nacre lights read as iridescent text, glow, border, highlight. Using a nacre light as a background fill on a dark ink collapses the system into the Paper Layer and destroys the iridescence entirely.
✓ Nacre as: text · outline · border · glow · pip · highlight · spectrum bar  ✗ Nacre as: background-color on dark ink

§ P.11.2 · four new semantic roles
--S-syzygy · var(--quire) · #fff6ff
Syzygy — the alignment moment
The precise alignment of three independent lines of argument into a single actionable configuration. In colour grammar: the one nacre that appears without preference across all three dark inks — barely visible, universally present. The convergence marker.
Token: --quire / #fff6ff · Use: alignment span · convergence marker · ambient on any dark ink · never as pip fill
--S-synthesis · var(--aqua-vitae) · #edfff8
Synthesis — the composite whole
The resolution that contains and transcends the parts. The determination that does not yet exist — that the Bill would mandate, that the process would produce. Cool mint at the centre of the nacre spectrum, bridging warm and cold.
Token: --aqua-vitae / #edfff8 · Use: confirmed synthesis · issued determination · completed derivation · pip ✦ on Cosmic Black
--S-dyadic · var(--deckle) · #dbccd7
Dyadic — the essential pair
The two-element structure from which the system derives: citizen and state, claim and demonstration, nacre and ink, paper and darkness. Deckle sits exactly between the two poles of the nacre spectrum. The colour of the binary itself — the relationship between two terms, not either term alone.
Token: --deckle / #dbccd7 · Use: binary status · paired comparison · two-column divider · pip ✦ on Blue Black
--S-bridge · var(--willow) · #d5e9db
Bridge — the mediating register
The crossing point. Willow is the only nacre that appears in all three ink registers — quiet on Grape, ambient on Cosmic, secondary on Blue Black. Always present, never primary, always connecting. The three-court architecture as colour: each jurisdiction covering what the others cannot reach.
Token: --willow / #d5e9db · Use: cross-register transitions · connector text · linking element · secondary ◈ on Limo
§ P.11.3 · grammar rules
Rule I
Nacre lights are not grounds on dark inks
On Cloud Dancer they are grounds. On the Mixed Inks they are luminous accents. Never use a nacre light as background-color on a dark ink.
✓ text · border · pip · glow   ✗ background-color on dark
Rule II
Follow the ancestry pairing
Each dark ink has a primary nacre whose ancestry aligns with the ink's hue. Grape → Pearl. Cosmic → Ice. Limo → Amber. Cross-pairing permitted for contrast.
✦ Primary: follow ancestry   ◈ complement   · ambient only
Rule III
One ink per section
The three Mixed Inks are semantically distinct. Do not mix them within the same panel. Adjacent panels may use different inks.
Adjacent sections: ✓   Same panel background: ✗
Rule IV
Shimmer is a ::before convention
Apply the CSS nacre shimmer to ::before pseudo-elements only. Radial gradient of primary nacre at opacity 0.05–0.12. Never on content.
Duration: 18–26s · Ease-in-out · Opacity: 0.05–0.12 only
Rule V
P.11 and the core grammar are compatible
Abalone is a themed layer over the existing system, not a replacement. Core semantic tokens still apply; nacre equivalents preferred within Abalone sections.
Core in Abalone: ✓ permitted   Nacre equivalents: ✦ preferred
Rule VI · Extended
Four new roles map to Abalone nacre
Syzygy · Synthesis · Dyadic · Bridge are structural roles first described here but applicable corpus-wide where their conditions are met.
