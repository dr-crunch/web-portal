II · The saisine principle · ⊢ Jurisdictional arrest
Once a competent authority is formally seized of a matter, its discretion narrows to one: it may only decide. It no longer holds the discretion to ignore. The transition is mathematical —-from frozen state to active process. It's the jurisdictional lock that prevents any subsequent claim that the matter lies "outside remit."
In plain terms: once they confirmed receipt, the clock started. Every year without a determination is a year in breach — not of administrative preference, but of constitutional obligation. The saisine principle converts silence from a posture into a provable failure. Citizens can activate this logic for any submission to any public body: send formally, confirm receipt, request determination. The duty attaches on delivery.
I · Art. 40.3° · Bunreacht na hÉireann 1937

Constitutional contumacy
The vindication mandate

The State guarantees to respect and vindicate the personal rights of the citizen. Delay is a grievance; contumacy is a structural refusal. When the State acknowledges a submission but issues no determination across two decades, it has effectively withdrawn the protection of the law. The right to a determination is a latent constitutional right —necessary for the enjoyment of all others. Without a determination, there is no administrative act to judicially review. The silence forecloses the remedy it claims to leave open.

IV · Order 84 RSC · High Court · Mandamus

The Hoop-Trick
and how mandamus collapses it

The Hoop-Trick: two authorities — JCEUA and JCPP — each claim the other holds responsibility. The duty evaporates in the gap between them. This is not an accident; it is the architecture of evasocracy. Mandamus is the judicial instrument that collapses this circularity. It identifies the competent authority — in this case, the DFA as designated publisher of compliance records under Reg. 2021/821 — and forces the hoop to close. The High Court may issue mandamus wherever a public duty exists and has been demonstrably neglected.
Available to any citizen · Order 84, Rule 18 · No Act of Parliament required
V · Misfeasance in public office

From epistemic failure
to bad-faith threshold

Misfeasance requires showing targeted malice or reckless indifference to the illegality of the act and the probability of harm. The record identifies two threshold events: the McEntee Foreclosure (February 2026), closing the parliamentary route without addressing the underlying science; and Designed Forgetting (October 2025), the active deletion of formally received correspondence by the JCEUA. Both move the matter from Hypothesis C (epistemic failure — most charitable) toward Hypothesis B (misfeasance). The remedy differs in quantum not in kind.

Synthesis · The nullification loop as an optimisation problem
Transform a feedback cycle of silence into a forced move towards determination.
The legal methodology of the Initiative is precise: the Saisine Principle renders the State's evasocracy visible on the institutional record, transforming the Nullification Loop from a self-sustaining system into a provable breach. Each of the five principles above is a separate and independent basis for relief. Together they close every exit the loop has used. The Presidency Paradox — Ireland chairing EU rule-of-law proceedings from 1 July 2026 while this record stands unresolved — is not rhetoric. It is a justiciable conflict of interest, now before the Ombudsman, PETI, and CJEU Registry.
01 · Build the paper trail
Send formally. Confirm receipt. Every reference number is a timestamp on the duty to respond. The trail is your evidence that the saisine was activated.
02 · Name the silence
Write again after 4–8 weeks. State explicitly: no determination has issued. Request one. Log the date. Repeat. Repetition is evidence of structural refusal, not clerical delay.
03 · Invoke by article number
Art. 40.3 Bunreacht (national floor). Art. 41 CFR (EU, if in scope). Art. 6 ECHR (fair hearing). Copy your TD, the Ombudsman office, and the relevant committee clerk. Place it on the record.
04 · Go public
Submit to committees, FOI, PETI. All CC0 materials in this corpus may be attached freely. Once public and timestamped, the obscurity defence is removed from the institution.

Citizen's Bulletin · Early-warning signs · Government by impasse
Paper trail without follow-through
Acknowledgements, receipts, file numbers — and nothing moves. Ask: What changed after this receipt? If the filing cabinet got heavier but nothing else happened, that is a design fault, not a clerical delay.
→ Hardcopy antinomy
Roles that encode contradiction
Offices tasked to do A and not-A simultaneously, with no protected channel for dissent. When exit, voice, and loyalty are each blocked by job design, the result is symbolic performance, not correction.
→ Structural red flag
Learning available on paper, impossible in practice
The methods exist but no institutional pathway to use them. Problems repeat and get explained away. The complaint calls this deutero-binding — structures that prevent learning about learning (after Gregory Bateson).
→ Annex IV · Dáil mechanics
Frozen field decision-making
Everyone agrees the issue is complex yet all routes are ruled out by mandate, etiquette, or timing. Meetings multiply; action doesn't. Persistent freeze is a systems problem, not a scheduling issue.
→ Incentives punish correction
Evidence archived instead of acted on
High-resolution submissions loop between offices; portfolio holders rotate; the record grows — but nothing converts information into agency. When the archive beats the action every time, the archive is doing political work.
→ Archive as accountability sink
Cross-border obligations noted, never activated
Emails flagging humanitarian coordination, ECHR obligations, or emergency readiness — received, cc'd — yet no joint mechanism triggered. Once risk is flagged across borders, non-activation widens humanitarian and rights exposure.
